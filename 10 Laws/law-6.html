06

Law of the Uncertainty Principle → Knowledge Limits

“Limits to precision are not bugs of reality but boundaries that preserve meaning, agency, and the necessity of trust.”

Δx · Δp ≥ ħ / 2

Conjugate quantities cannot be simultaneously specified with arbitrary precision. Epistemic grain creates space for faith and humility.

The Physics Foundation: Heisenberg Limits

  • Conjugates: Position–momentum, time–energy exhibit reciprocal uncertainties.
  • No Hidden Classicality: Limits are intrinsic, not merely instrumental.
  • Trade-offs: More clarity here implies less clarity there.

The Spiritual Parallel

“Now we see through a glass, darkly…”
— 1 Corinthians 13:12

Knowledge is bounded so that love, trust, and moral risk remain meaningful. If reality were exhaustively knowable in advance, freedom would be trivial and virtue impossible.

Properties

  • Humility: Certainty is contextual, not absolute.
  • Prudence: Decisions under uncertainty demand wisdom.
  • Hope: The veil is partial, not total.

Implications

  • Ethics: Responsibility persists despite incomplete knowledge.
  • Theology: Mystery is principled, not ad hoc obscurity.
  • Practice: Evidence + faith are complementary, not rivals.
